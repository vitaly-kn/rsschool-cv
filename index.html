André-Marie Ampère

The Newton of Electricity

Statue of Ampère in Lyon

Here's a time line of Ampère's life:

  • 1775 - Born in Lyon (France)
  • 1799 - took his first regular job as a mathematics teacher
  • 1804 - moved to Paris to become a tutor at the École Polytechnique
  • 1809 - became professor of mathematics at the École Polytechnique
  • 1814 - the new Institut Impériale, which would eventually become part of the Academy of Sciences, invited Ampère to join
  • 1827 - published his magnum opus "Memoir on the Mathematical Theory of Electrodynamic Phenomena, Uniquely Deduced from Experience", the work that coined the name of his new science, electrodynamics
  • 1827 - was elected a foreign member of the Royal Society (United Kingdom)
  • 1828 - was eleceted a foreign member of the Royal Swedish Academy of Science
  • 1836 - died in Paris. He is buried in the Montmartre cemetery

"The experimental investigation by which Ampere established the law of the mechanical action between electric currents is one of the most brilliant achievements in science. The whole theory and experiment, seems as if it had leaped, full grown and full armed, from the brain of the “Newton of Electricity”. It is perfect in form, and unassailable in accuracy, and it is summed up in a formula from which all the phenomena may be deduced, and which must always remain the cardinal formula of electro-dynamics."

-- James Clerk Maxwell
